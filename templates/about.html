About

A year is the time it takes for a planet to revolve around the sun. Further, a day is the time it takes for a planet to turn once around its rotational axis.

We use 62138156134 (magic constant) + unix timestamp (seconds since unix epoch) to calculate the total number of seconds that have passed since 1.1.0001 AD.

Given the orbital period in Hz we can calculate the current year:

n_years = s_passed / orbital_period.

Days are calculated from the remainder of this division:

n_days = (remainder * orbital_period) / len_day

All orbital data was obtained from: http://ssd.jpl.nasa.gov/horizons.cgi
